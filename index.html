FAQ

Common Garage Door Questions

How fast can you come to repair my garage door in Needham?
We offer same-day service in Needham and most of Greater Boston. In most cases we can have a technician at your home within 1–3 hours of your call. For after-hours emergencies, call us — we prioritize security calls (door stuck open or won't close).
How much does it cost to fix a broken garage door spring?
Single torsion spring replacement typically runs $250–$450 in the Boston area, including parts and labor. We always recommend replacing springs in pairs to prevent a quick re-call. You'll get a free written estimate before any work begins.
Are you licensed and insured in Massachusetts?
Yes. AK Garage Door Pro is a fully licensed Home Improvement Contractor in MA and carries $1M general liability insurance plus workers compensation. We can provide proof on request — and we recommend never hiring a garage door company that can't.
Which garage door opener brands do you install?
We install LiftMaster, Chamberlain, and Genie openers — including Wi-Fi smart models that connect to your phone. Most installs take 2–3 hours and come with full manufacturer warranty plus our workmanship guarantee.
Do you offer 24/7 emergency garage door repair?
We offer 7 days a week service from 7am–8pm with no overtime fees on weekends. For after-hours emergencies (door stuck open, won't close, security risk), call (857) 468-1852 — we prioritize these calls.
What areas around Boston do you serve?
We provide service in Boston and surrounding areas — including Needham, Newton, Wellesley, Dedham, Westwood, Natick, Brookline, Norwood, Waltham, and other Greater Boston communities, generally within a 25-mile radius.
Can you replace just one panel of my garage door?
Yes — if your door is in good shape and only one or two panels are dented or damaged, we can color-match and replace individual sections. This typically saves you $1,000+ versus replacing the whole door.
How long does a garage door last?
A quality steel door lasts 20–30 years with normal use. Springs last 10,000–20,000 cycles (about 7–10 years for most homes). Openers last 10–15 years. An annual tune-up extends the life of every component significantly.
